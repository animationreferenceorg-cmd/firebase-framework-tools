Interview Meet the Instructor Question 01 How did you first get into video production? I first experienced video production during college, when I was working part-time at a game development company. While the work itself was simple enough for even an inexperienced student like me, it introduced me to the fundamentals of video production, eventually becoming the starting point of my career. Question 02 What did you struggle with when studying this field, and what was your strategy to overcome that? What I especially focused on was the balance between quality and speed. Early in my career, I had a hard time meeting the required quality; I had to retake and modify my scenes so often that it took very long to complete them. Consequently, I decided to study high-quality anime works, playing the best scenes again and again to learn their timing and layouts and apply them to my own projects. These efforts allowed me to gradually reduce retakes while maintaining a steadier production pace. Question 03 What is the biggest challenge faced by those studying this field or hesitating to take this class, and how can this class help them overcome it? Producing and directing a video comes with a wide range of difficulties. These include setting the initial direction, determining where to start the animation process, and finding the right timing for making final changes. But they cannot be solved simply by following a textbook; you need the right approach. Although there is no single correct answer in directing, this class demonstrates how to overcome these challenges by breaking them down into small, manageable parts, based on my unique approach and work process.
